# Rules

There are many variations of Hnefatafl throughout history, this game uses a variation of the [Copenhagen rules](https://aagenielsen.dk/copenhagen_rules.php) outlined in 2012 by Aage Nielsen, Adam Bartley, and Tim Millar.

## Summary

On player assumes the role of the Attack and the other the Defender.

Pieces are setup on an 11x11 board.

The Attacker begins with 24 pieces, 6 on each edge of the board.

The Defender begins with 1 King at the center of the board, and with 12 standard pieces surrounding the King.

The Attacker always begins, with players taking turns moving pieces until the King is either captured or escapes from on the board corners.

## Movement

A piece may move any number of spaces orthogonally without moving through other pieces.

## Capturing

A standard opposing piece is captured if your move ends with you sandwiching a piece between a moved piece either a Restricted Space, or another one of your own pieces.

Since pieces are only captured during your own movement, you may move between two opposing pieces without being captured.

The King may participate in capturing opposing pieces.

The King is only captured if trapped on all 4 sides.

## Restricted Spaces

The center space and the 4 corners may only be occupied by the King, though other pieces may pass through them.

Empty restricted spaces may act as an opposing piece for the purpose of capturing.

## Defender Win

The Defender wins if their King moves into a corner space.

## Attacker Win

The Attacker wins if they capture the King.

## Automatic Loss

If either player is unable to move on their turn, they automatically lose.
